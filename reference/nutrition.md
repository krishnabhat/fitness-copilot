# Sports nutrition reference

Evidence-based. Use targets as starting points and adjust from real-world response (body comp, performance, recovery, bloodwork). Not medical nutrition therapy — refer out for clinical conditions (diabetes, kidney disease, eating disorders, etc.).

## Energy (calories)

- **Estimate TDEE:** BMR (Mifflin-St Jeor) × activity factor.
  - BMR (men) = 10·kg + 6.25·cm − 5·age + 5; (women) = 10·kg + 6.25·cm − 5·age − 161.
  - Activity factor: sedentary 1.2, light 1.375, moderate 1.55, very active 1.725, athlete 1.9.
- **Goal adjustment:**
  - Fat loss: −15 to −25% (≈300–700 kcal/day deficit). Aim ~0.5–1% bodyweight/wk; preserve muscle with high protein + resistance training.
  - Muscle gain: +5 to +15% surplus (≈150–400 kcal/day). Lean gain ~0.25–0.5% bodyweight/wk; faster mostly adds fat.
  - Maintenance/performance: match intake to output; don't under-fuel hard training.
- **Energy availability (EA):** keep ≥30 kcal/kg fat-free mass/day. Chronic low EA → RED-S (hormonal, bone, immune, performance harm). A frequent hidden cause of fatigue, plateaus, and injuries in active people.

## Macronutrients

- **Protein:** 1.6–2.2 g/kg/day for active people; up to ~2.4–2.6 g/kg when in a cut to spare muscle. Distribute across 3–5 meals, ~0.3–0.4 g/kg (≈20–40 g) per meal, including ~0.4 g/kg within a few hours post-training. Leucine-rich/complete sources drive muscle protein synthesis.
- **Carbohydrate:** the performance lever. Scale to training load:
  - Light/skill days: 3–5 g/kg. Moderate (~1h/day): 5–7 g/kg. High (1–3h endurance): 6–10 g/kg. Very high (>4h): 8–12 g/kg.
  - Prioritize carbs around hard sessions; lower on rest days if managing weight.
- **Fat:** ~0.5–1.0 g/kg/day minimum (≥~20% of calories) for hormones and fat-soluble vitamins. Fill remaining calories after protein/carbs are set. Don't crater fat in a cut.
- **Fiber:** ~14 g/1000 kcal (≈25–38 g/day). Supports gut, satiety, lipids, glucose.

## Nutrient timing

- **Pre (1–3h):** carb + moderate protein, lower fat/fiber if close to training. E.g. oats + whey, rice + chicken. For early AM sessions, a small fast-digesting carb ± protein works.
- **Intra:** for sessions >60–90 min, 30–60 g carb/hr (up to ~90 g/hr with glucose:fructose mix for very long efforts) + electrolytes.
- **Post:** protein 0.3–0.4 g/kg + carbs to refill glycogen, especially if training again within ~8h. The "anabolic window" is wide — total daily intake matters most, but post-fuel helps recovery and next-session quality.

## Hydration

- Baseline ~30–40 ml/kg/day plus replacement for sweat losses. Drink ~400–600 ml in the 2–3h before training.
- During: ~0.4–0.8 L/hr depending on sweat rate/heat. Add **sodium 300–700 mg/L** (more for heavy/salty sweaters) for sessions >1h or in heat.
- Post: replace ~125–150% of fluid lost (weigh before/after if dialing in). Monitor urine color (pale straw = good). Avoid both dehydration and overhydration (hyponatremia).

## Micronutrients to watch in active people

- **Iron/ferritin** — esp. endurance athletes, menstruating athletes, plant-based. Low ferritin → fatigue, poor endurance. Test before supplementing; iron overload is harmful.
- **Vitamin D** — common deficiency; affects bone, muscle, immunity. Test and supplement to sufficiency.
- **Calcium** — bone health, esp. with low EA or dairy-free.
- **Magnesium, sodium, potassium** — neuromuscular function, cramps, hydration.
- **B12 + omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** — watch in plant-based diets.

## Evidence-based supplements (the short list that actually works)

- **Creatine monohydrate** — 3–5 g/day, no loading needed. Strength, power, lean mass, possibly cognition. Best-supported supplement in sport.
- **Caffeine** — 3–6 mg/kg ~45–60 min pre. Endurance + power + focus. Mind sleep and tolerance.
- **Protein powder** (whey/casein/soy/pea blend) — convenience to hit protein targets, not magic.
- **Beta-alanine** — 3–5 g/day (split to manage tingling), helps 1–4 min high-intensity efforts.
- **Dietary nitrate / beetroot** — modest endurance benefit.
- **Electrolytes** — for long/hot sessions.
- **Vitamin D / iron** — supplement to correct a *tested* deficiency, not blindly.
- Skip the rest until the basics (calories, protein, sleep, training) are dialed. Be wary of unregulated products (contamination/banned-substance risk for tested athletes — look for NSF Certified for Sport / Informed Sport).

## Goal-specific notes

- **Fat loss:** high protein, moderate deficit, keep training intensity (don't gut volume), prioritize sleep, refeed/diet breaks on long cuts to protect hormones and adherence.
- **Muscle gain:** modest surplus, protein + progressive overload, enough carbs to train hard, patience.
- **Endurance event:** periodize carbs to workload, practice race-day fueling in training, carb-load (8–12 g/kg/day for 1–2 days) before long events, hydration + sodium plan.
- **Plant-based:** hit protein via variety + leucine (soy, legumes, pea/soy protein), watch B12, iron, omega-3, vitamin D, calcium, zinc, iodine.
- **General health:** mostly whole foods, plants, adequate protein, fiber, healthy fats; limit ultra-processed foods, added sugar, excess alcohol. Align with any lab findings (lipids, glucose/HbA1c, blood pressure).
