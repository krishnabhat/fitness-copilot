# Equipment profiles — adapt the plan to what's actually available

Same goal, different tools. Read the athlete's `equipment` / `location` from the
profile (or ask) and pick exercises that fit. Never prescribe a lift they can't
load. When swapping, preserve the **movement pattern, intensity, and stimulus** —
just change the implement.

## The six patterns to cover (program around these, not around machines)
Squat · Hinge · Horizontal push · Horizontal pull · Vertical push · Vertical pull
(+ carries, core, and single-leg). Pick the best available tool for each.

## Full commercial gym
- Everything available: barbells, racks, full dumbbell range, cables, machines, specialty bars, sleds, cardio equipment.
- **Lean into:** barbell strength lifts with safeties, cable/machine isolation for hypertrophy with easy load micro-loading, leg press/hack squat as squat-pattern volume, sled/rower/bike for conditioning.
- Progression is easy (small plates, pin stacks) — use double progression and precise load jumps.

## Garage / home gym (typical: barbell + plates, rack with safeties, bench, some dumbbells/kettlebells, maybe bands, pull-up bar)
- **Strength is fully covered** by the barbell — squat, bench, deadlift, press, rows, pull-ups.
- **Gaps to engineer around:**
  - Limited dumbbell range → use bands, tempo, pauses, 1.5-reps, or higher reps to drive stimulus without heavier DBs.
  - No cable stack → bands (face pulls, pressdowns, lat work), DB/landmine variations, or barbell row variants.
  - No leg machines → barbell + DB/KB: front squats, RDLs, split squats, hip thrusts off a bench, walking lunges.
  - Conditioning → KB swings, EMOMs, jump rope, hill sprints outside, carries, circuits.
- Use safeties/spotter arms so the athlete can train close to failure solo.

## Home minimal (adjustable dumbbells &/or kettlebells, bench or none, bands, pull-up bar)
- **Drive intensity through proximity-to-failure, tempo, unilateral work, and density** since load is capped.
- Squat: goblet/DB front-rack squats, Bulgarian split squats, step-ups. Hinge: DB/KB RDLs, single-leg RDLs, KB swings, hip thrusts. Push: DB bench/floor press, push-ups (deficit/weighted/feet-elevated), DB/landmine OHP. Pull: pull-ups/rows on a bar, DB rows, band pull-aparts. Carries with the heaviest implement.
- Add bands for accommodating resistance and to extend a light dumbbell.

## Bodyweight / travel (no equipment, hotel room, outdoors)
- Push-up & row progressions (towel/door rows, or find a bar), pistol/Bulgarian/Cossack squats, single-leg RDLs, glute bridges/hip thrusts, nordic/eccentric hamstring, planks, hollow holds, mountain climbers, burpees.
- **Progress by leverage and tempo, not load:** harder variations, slower eccentrics, pauses, 1.5-reps, AMRAP density, shorter rest, plyometrics.
- Conditioning: running, stairs, sprints, jump rope, EMOM/Tabata bodyweight circuits.
- Mobility/yoga needs nothing — great travel option.

## Cardio / endurance equipment swaps
- No track → road/treadmill; no treadmill → outdoor; bike/row/elliptical/ski-erg all substitute for Zone 2 and intervals. Match by RPE/HR zone, not by machine.

## Outdoor / field
- Hill sprints, tempo runs, sled/prowler if available, sandbag/odd-object carries and cleans, plyometrics, calisthenics on a pull-up bar/bench.

## Substitution quick-reference (preserve the pattern)
| Need | Full gym | Garage | Minimal / bodyweight |
|---|---|---|---|
| Squat | Back squat, leg press, hack | Front/back squat, split squat | Goblet, Bulgarian, pistol |
| Hinge | Deadlift, RDL, GHR, leg curl | Barbell RDL, KB swing, hip thrust | SL-RDL, KB swing, nordic, bridge |
| H-push | Bench, machine press | Barbell/DB bench, floor press | Push-up variations |
| H-pull | Cable/seated row, chest-supported | Barbell row, DB row | Inverted/door/towel row |
| V-push | OHP, machine shoulder press | Barbell/DB OHP, landmine | Pike/HSPU progression, band press |
| V-pull | Lat pulldown, pull-up | Pull-up, band pulldown | Pull-up / band-assisted |
| Conditioning | Rower, bike, sled, treadmill | KB, jump rope, hill sprints | Run, burpees, Tabata circuits |

**Rule of thumb:** if the prescribed tool isn't available, drop one row in this
table to the athlete's column, keep the sets/reps/RPE intent, and note the swap
and why in the session's "Why today" line.
