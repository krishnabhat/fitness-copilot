# Training reference

Evidence-based programming detail. Read the relevant section when the task calls for it.

## First principles (apply to every modality)

- **Specificity** — adaptations are specific to the stimulus. Train the quality you want (strength, size, endurance, power, mobility).
- **Progressive overload** — to keep adapting, stress must trend upward over time via load, reps, sets, density, ROM, tempo, or reduced rest. Don't progress every variable at once.
- **Fatigue management / recovery** — adaptation happens during recovery. Manage the fatigue-to-fitness ratio; more is not better past the point you can recover from.
- **Individualization** — age, training age, injury history, sleep, stress, genetics, and preferences all modulate the right dose.
- **Reversibility** — detraining is real but strength/skill return faster than they were built (muscle memory).
- **Variation, not randomness** — rotate exercises/intensities purposefully; chaos ≠ progress.

## Intensity & effort scales

- **%1RM ↔ reps (Epley-ish, individual variation):** 100%≈1, 95%≈2, 90%≈4, 85%≈6, 80%≈8, 75%≈10, 70%≈12, 67%≈15, 60%≈20.
- **RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion, 6–10):** RPE 10 = no reps left; 9 = 1 left; 8 = 2 left; 7 = 3 left.
- **RIR (Reps In Reserve):** RIR = 10 − RPE. Most hypertrophy work lives at RIR 0–3; most strength singles/doubles at RPE 7–9.
- **Autoregulation:** prescribe by RPE/RIR (or % with an RPE cap) so daily readiness sets the load. On a bad-readiness day, hold reps and drop a set or lower the RPE cap.

## Periodization

- **Linear:** intensity ↑, volume ↓ over weeks. Simple, good for beginners and strength peaks.
- **Undulating (DUP):** vary rep ranges within a week (e.g. heavy / moderate / light days). Good for intermediates, joint-friendly variety.
- **Block:** sequenced phases — accumulation (volume) → intensification (load) → realization/peak/taper. Good for athletes peaking for an event.
- **Mesocycle structure:** 3–6 week accumulation building volume, then a **deload** (≈40–60% volume or intensity drop) to dissipate fatigue. Deload triggers: stalled lifts, persistent soreness, poor sleep, mood/motivation dip, joints aching.

## Strength & power

- **Goal:** maximal force. **Dose:** 3–6+ sets of 1–6 reps at 80–95%+ 1RM (RPE 7–9), 3–5 min rest. Frequency 2–4×/wk per lift.
- Prioritize big compound lifts (squat, hinge/deadlift, bench/press, row, pull-up). Technique under load is the limiter — leave reps in reserve on most sets, save true maxes for testing.
- **Power/explosive:** 3–5 sets of 1–5 fast reps at 30–60% 1RM (or Olympic lifts/jumps/throws), full recovery (2–5 min). Quality over fatigue — stop when bar speed drops.

## Hypertrophy (muscle size)

- **Dose:** 6–20+ reps, sets taken to RIR 0–3, 30–90s+ rest (longer for compounds). Effort proximity to failure matters more than exact rep number.
- **Weekly volume landmarks per muscle (hard sets/wk):** MEV (minimum effective) ≈ 6–10; productive range ≈ 10–20; MRV (max recoverable) ≈ ~20+ (individual). Start low end, add sets as you recover/progress, deload when you hit MRV.
- **Frequency:** hit each muscle 2×/wk to distribute volume. Manage with full-body, upper/lower, or push/pull/legs splits.
- **Progression:** add reps within the range, then load when top of range is hit (double progression).

## HIIT / metabolic conditioning

- **What it is:** repeated short hard efforts (≥80–90% max HR or RPE 8–10) with incomplete recovery. Time-efficient for VO2max and metabolic health.
- **Formats:** 30s on / 30s off; Tabata (20s/10s × 8); 4×4 min @ ~90–95% HRmax / 3 min easy (classic VO2max norwegian protocol); EMOMs; circuits.
- **Dosing:** intense — cap at ~1–3 sessions/wk for most; keep away from heavy lower-body strength days and from key endurance quality days. Quality collapses fast; stop when power/form degrade.
- **Contraindications:** uncontrolled cardiovascular disease/hypertension, certain injuries. Screen first (see sports-medicine red flags).

### HIIT is not just running/biking — rotate the modality
A HIIT day can be a cardio-machine session OR a dumbbell/bodyweight metabolic workout. Vary it week to week for a better total-body stimulus and more muscle retention on a recomp. Menu:

- **Bodyweight:** squat jumps, mountain climbers, high knees, skaters, burpees, push-ups, jumping jacks, shuttle runs. Great for travel.
- **Dumbbell metcon (adjustable DBs + bench):** thrusters, goblet squats, push press, floor press, reverse lunges, step-ups, DB snatch, devil's press, renegade rows, gorilla rows, carries.
- **Formats to cycle:** EMOM (every minute on the minute), AMRAP (as many rounds as possible in X min), Tabata (20s/10s ×8), 30/30 intervals, **DB complexes** (string 4–5 lifts with one pair of DBs, no rest until the round is done), and classic bike/hill/rower intervals.

**Spine-safe HIIT (for a disc history):** under fatigue, avoid loaded spinal flexion and ballistic hinging (heavy DB swings/cleans/snatches if the back is iffy). Favor squat / press / lunge / step-up / carry patterns and machine/bike intervals on days the lower back is fatigued. Crisp technique only — stop the set the moment form degrades, not when you're maximally gassed.

**Heart-smart HIIT (cardiac/cholesterol/statin):** thorough warm-up, ramp intensity over the first rounds, breathe continuously (no Valsalva), and treat any chest discomfort/dizziness as a stop. Cap ~1–2 hard HIIT sessions/week, not back-to-back with heavy lower-body days.

**Example garage sessions (~25–30 min, spine-safe, scalable):**
- *DB EMOM 20 min* — rotate each minute: min1 10 goblet squats · min2 10 DB push press · min3 12 step-ups (6/leg) · min4 10 DB floor press. Rest the remainder of each minute; 5 rounds.
- *Bodyweight Tabata ×3 blocks* — Block A squats, Block B mountain climbers, Block C push-ups (20s on/10s off ×8 each, 1 min between blocks).
- *Spine-safe DB complex* — 5 rounds, minimal rest: 8 goblet squats → 8 push press → 8 reverse lunges (4/leg) → 8 floor press. Rest 90s between rounds.
- *Carry + bike finisher* — 5 rounds: 40 m farmer carry + 45s hard bike + 45s easy.

## Endurance running & cardio

- **Polarized / 80-20:** ~80% of training easy (Zone 2, conversational), ~20% hard (threshold/VO2max). Most people run their easy too hard and hard too easy.
- **HR zones (% HRmax; estimate HRmax≈208−0.7×age or use measured/LTHR):**
  - Z1 50–60% recovery; **Z2 60–70% easy aerobic base (most volume here)**; Z3 70–80% tempo/"gray zone"; Z4 80–90% threshold; Z5 90–100% VO2max.
- **Key sessions:** long easy run (aerobic base), tempo/threshold (comfortably hard, 20–40 min), VO2max intervals (e.g. 5×3 min hard / equal easy), strides.
- **Progression:** grow weekly mileage ~10%/wk max, with a down week every 3–4 weeks. Build volume before intensity.
- **Strength for runners:** 2×/wk heavy-ish lower-body + core improves economy and durability without bulk.

## Yoga / mobility / flexibility

- **Mobility = usable range under control;** flexibility = passive range. Train both: end-range strength (e.g. ATG split squats, Jefferson curls light, controlled articular rotations) plus stretching.
- **Static stretching:** best post-session or standalone (30–60s holds). Avoid long static holds immediately *before* max strength/power work (transient strength dip); use dynamic mobility instead pre-lift.
- **Yoga styles:** Vinyasa/Power (dynamic, conditioning + mobility), Hatha (slower, alignment), Yin (long passive holds, connective tissue), Restorative (parasympathetic recovery). Use Yin/Restorative as active recovery between hard days.
- **Programming:** 2–4 mobility/yoga touches per week; daily 5–10 min targeted drills for specific restrictions (e.g. ankles, hips, t-spine, shoulders). Breath-led, no bouncing into pain.

## Time-capped programming (fit the session to the clock)

The athlete's available time varies day to day. Scale the session to the budget — don't just rush a full plan. Budget the *whole* session: warm-up + main work + rest + cooldown must fit.

**Priority order — fill time from the top, cut from the bottom:**
1. Brief specific warm-up (always keep, even if 3–5 min).
2. The 1–2 highest-leverage main lifts/efforts toward today's goal.
3. Secondary compound / primary accessory.
4. Isolation / "nice to have" accessories.
5. Extra conditioning.
6. Extended cooldown/mobility (keep a short version always).

**Levers to compress time without killing the stimulus:**
- **Supersets/antagonist pairing** (e.g. push + pull) — rest one muscle while working another; near-halves wasted rest.
- **Circuits / EMOM / AMRAP** — fixed time blocks, built-in density.
- **Fewer exercises, more quality sets** — 2–3 exercises taken closer to failure beats 6 rushed ones.
- **Trim rest selectively** — keep full rest (2–4 min) on heavy compounds where it protects performance/safety; shorten rest (30–75s) on accessories and metabolic work.
- **Drop sets / rest-pause / myo-reps** — accumulate effective reps in less time on isolation work.
- **Shrink ROM-warmup, not safety-warmup** — never skip ramping into heavy loads.

**Rough templates by budget (general training):**
- **15–20 min:** 5 min warm-up → one main lift OR one full-body superset/EMOM → 2 min cooldown. Pick the single biggest bang.
- **30 min:** 5–7 min warm-up → 1 main lift (3–4 sets) + 1 antagonist superset → short cooldown.
- **45 min:** 8 min warm-up → 2 main lifts + 1–2 accessory supersets → cooldown.
- **60 min:** full warm-up → 2–3 main lifts + accessories → conditioning option + cooldown.
- **75–90 min:** full warm-up → strength + accessories + conditioning + extended mobility; room for longer rest on heavy work.
- **Endurance:** scale duration directly — short = quality intervals or a brisk Z2; long = aerobic base. Keep ~10 min easy bookends.

Always set the session's `duration_min` to the real budget and tell the athlete what you cut and why if time forced trade-offs.

## Session templates

**Warm-up (general → specific, ~8–12 min):** 3–5 min easy cardio → dynamic mobility for the day's patterns → activation (e.g. glutes/scaps) → 2–4 ramping warm-up sets to working load.

**Cooldown:** 3–5 min easy movement to bring HR down → light static stretching/breathing for primary muscles → quick log of how it felt (RPE, pain, energy).

## Weekly structure heuristics

- **Recovery between hard efforts for the same muscle:** ~48h. Stagger so consecutive days don't hammer the same tissue.
- **Don't stack** heavy lower-body strength + hard HIIT + a long run on back-to-back days. Sequence hard/easy.
- **Minimum effective dose beats heroics:** 3–4 quality sessions you recover from > 6 you don't.
- **Microcycle example (general fitness):** Mon strength (upper), Tue Z2 cardio + mobility, Wed strength (lower), Thu HIIT or intervals, Fri yoga/recovery, Sat long easy cardio or full-body, Sun off.
